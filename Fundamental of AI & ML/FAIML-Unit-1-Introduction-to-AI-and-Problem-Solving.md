## 1. Turing Test

The Turing Test was proposed by Alan Turing in 1950 in his paper “Computing Machinery and Intelligence”.

It provides an operational way to answer the question: “Can machines think?”

---

### The Imitation Game

Participants:

* Machine (A)
* Human (B)
* Interrogator (C)

Setup:

* Interrogator communicates with both A and B using text only
* The goal is to identify which one is human

Passing Criteria:

* If the machine successfully fools the interrogator, it is considered intelligent

---

## 2. Capabilities Required for Turing Test

* Natural Language Processing (NLP) → to communicate
* Knowledge Representation → to store information
* Automated Reasoning → to answer questions
* Machine Learning → to adapt and learn

---

## 3. Criticism of Turing Test

Chinese Room Argument:

* A machine can simulate understanding without actually understanding meaning
* It behaves intelligently but lacks true comprehension

---

## 4. Approaches to Artificial Intelligence

AI is defined along two dimensions:

* Thinking vs Acting
* Human-like vs Rational

---

### Four Approaches:

1. Thinking Humanly (Cognitive Modeling)

* Mimics human thinking
* Based on cognitive science

---

2. Thinking Rationally (Laws of Thought)

* Uses logic to solve problems
* Example:

  * All men are mortal
  * Socrates is a man
  * Therefore, Socrates is mortal

---

3. Acting Humanly (Turing Test Approach)

* Focuses on behavior
* Machine behaves like a human

---

4. Acting Rationally (Rational Agent Approach)

* Agent acts to achieve best outcome
* Most modern approach

---

## 5. Techniques to Solve AI Problems

AI techniques organize and process knowledge efficiently.

### Properties of AI Knowledge:

* Huge volume of data
* Unstructured
* Dynamic (changes frequently)

---

### Requirements:

* Searchable
* Modifiable
* Understandable

---

## 6. Key AI Techniques

* Search → finding path from initial to goal state
* Knowledge Representation → storing facts and relationships
* Abstraction → simplifying complex problems

---

## 7. Levels of AI Modeling

---

### 1. Biological Level (Lowest)

* Simulates human brain structure
* Very complex

---

### 2. Cognitive Level (Middle)

* Simulates thinking process
* Focus on reasoning

---

### 3. Behavioral Level (Highest)

* Focus on output behavior
* Internal process ignored

---

## Conclusion:

Most AI systems today operate at cognitive or behavioral level.

---

## 8. Building an Intelligent System

To build an intelligent system, follow four phases:

---

### Phase 1: Define the Problem

* Initial state → starting point
* Goal state → desired output
* State space → all possible states
* Operators → actions to move between states

---

### Phase 2: Analyze the Problem

Check:

* Can it be divided into subproblems?
* Can steps be undone?
* Is the solution predictable?
* Do we need optimal solution?

---

### Phase 3: Knowledge Representation

* Organize knowledge properly
* Make it easy for system to process

---

### Phase 4: Choose Technique

* If optimal solution needed → algorithmic search
* If large data → heuristic search

---

## 9. Physical Symbol System Hypothesis (PSSH)

Proposed by Allen Newell and Herbert Simon.

Statement:
A physical symbol system has the necessary and sufficient means for general intelligent action.

---

### Key Ideas:

* Intelligence = symbol manipulation
* Symbols represent real-world objects
* System processes symbols to solve problems

---

## 10. Key Assumptions of AI

* Intelligence is information processing
* Thinking = symbol manipulation
* Intelligence is independent of biology
* Systems require internal representation
* Problem solving involves search

---

## 11. Criticism of Symbolic AI

* Human intelligence includes intuition
* Not everything can be represented using symbols
* Led to development of Neural Networks

---

## Summary

* Turing Test defines intelligence behaviorally
* AI has four main approaches
* Problem solving involves structured steps
* Symbolic AI focuses on logic and symbols
* Modern AI uses cognitive and behavioral models
